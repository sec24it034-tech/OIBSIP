Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

"Missile Man of India" | "People's President"

A Tribute to the Missile Man

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, more popularly known as Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, was one of India's most respected scientists and statesmen. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and played a pivotal role in India's space program and missile development. His life journey from a small town in Rameswaram to the highest office in the land is an inspiring tale of determination, hard work, and unwavering commitment to the nation.

Life Journey

1931

Humble Beginnings

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a humble boat owner. Despite financial constraints, his father instilled in him the values of honesty, perseverance, and service to others.

1955

Educational Excellence

Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and then earned a degree in Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.

1969

ISRO Journey

Joined ISRO and worked on various satellite launch vehicle projects. Later became the chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme.

1981

Padma Bhushan

Awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, for his outstanding contribution to science and technology.

2002

President of India

Elected as the 11th President of India, becoming the first scientist and the first Tamil to hold the highest constitutional office.

2015

Eternal Legacy

Passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. His death left the nation in mourning, but his legacy continues to inspire millions.

Major Achievements

🚀

Missile Development

Led the development of India's missile program, including Agni and Prithvi missiles, earning him the title "Missile Man of India."

🛰️

Space Research

Contributed significantly to India's space program, working on India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).

🏛️

Presidency

Served as the 11th President of India, known as the "People's President" for his approachable nature and connection with citizens.

📚

Educational Reform

Championed education reform and inspired millions of students through his lectures and books like "Wings of Fire."
